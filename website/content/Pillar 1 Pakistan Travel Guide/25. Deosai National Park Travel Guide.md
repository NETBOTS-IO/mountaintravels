Deosai National Park Travel Guide
Discover the Land of Giants
One of the World's Highest Alpine Plateaus
Located high above the valleys of Gilgit-Baltistan, Deosai National Park is one of Pakistan's most extraordinary natural wonders.
Known as the "Land of Giants," Deosai is a vast wilderness of rolling grasslands, crystal-clear streams, wildflowers, snow-capped mountains, and pristine lakes.
Situated at an average elevation of approximately 4,100 meters (13,450 feet), it is among the highest alpine plateaus in the world.
For travelers seeking dramatic landscapes, wildlife encounters, photography opportunities, and untouched natural beauty, Deosai offers an unforgettable experience unlike anywhere else in Pakistan.

What is Deosai?
Deosai National Park is a protected alpine ecosystem located between Skardu and Astore in Gilgit-Baltistan.
The park covers thousands of square kilometers of high-altitude plains and serves as one of Pakistan's most important conservation areas.
During summer, Deosai transforms into a breathtaking landscape of:
✓ Wildflowers
✓ Green meadows
✓ Alpine lakes
✓ Mountain streams
✓ Wildlife habitats
✓ Endless open horizons
The scenery often surprises visitors who expect only rugged mountains and glaciers in northern Pakistan.

The Legend of the Land of Giants
The name "Deosai" is often translated as "Land of Giants."
Local legends tell stories of mysterious giants who once inhabited these vast plains.
Whether myth or folklore, the name perfectly captures the immense scale and dramatic character of the landscape.
The seemingly endless grasslands and sweeping mountain views create an atmosphere unlike any other destination in Pakistan.

Sheosar Lake
Jewel of Deosai National Park
Sheosar Lake is one of the most famous attractions in Deosai.
Its name means:
"Blind Lake"
in the local language.
Situated amidst the open plains, the lake reflects the surrounding mountains and skies, creating exceptional photographic opportunities.
On clear days, visitors may enjoy distant views of:
Nanga Parbat
the world's ninth-highest mountain.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Deosai worth visiting?
Absolutely. Many travelers consider it one of Pakistan's most beautiful natural landscapes.

How many days should I spend in Deosai?
A day trip is possible, but an overnight stay provides a more immersive experience.

When is Deosai open?
Typically during the summer months, depending on snowfall and road conditions.

Can I see wildlife?
Yes. Marmots are common, and other wildlife species may also be observed.

Can Deosai be combined with Hunza and Skardu?
Yes. It is one of the most popular extensions to a Skardu itinerary.
