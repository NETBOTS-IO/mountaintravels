Complete Astore Travel Guide
Everything You Need to Know About Visiting Astore Valley, Pakistan
Discover One of Gilgit-Baltistan's Hidden Gems
Located in the southern part of Gilgit-Baltistan, Astore Valley is one of Pakistan's most scenic and least crowded mountain destinations.
Known for its alpine meadows, crystal-clear lakes, lush valleys, traditional villages, and proximity to Deosai National Park and Nanga Parbat, Astore offers travelers an authentic mountain experience away from the crowds.
Whether you are seeking nature, photography, adventure, camping, cultural experiences, or a peaceful escape, Astore is one of northern Pakistan's most rewarding destinations.

Where Is Astore Valley?
Astore Valley
Astore is located southeast of Gilgit and serves as an important gateway to Deosai National Park and parts of the Nanga Parbat region.
Approximate distances:
Gilgit to Astore: 120 km 
Skardu to Astore (via Deosai in season) 
Islamabad to Astore: approximately 10–12 hours by road 
The valley is surrounded by spectacular mountains, forests, rivers, and alpine landscapes.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is Astore famous for?
Astore is famous for Rama Meadows, Rama Lake, Deosai access, Minimarg, and spectacular mountain scenery.

Is Astore worth visiting?
Absolutely. It is one of the most beautiful and less-crowded destinations in Gilgit-Baltistan.

What is the best time to visit Astore?
Generally from June to September.

Can Astore be combined with Skardu?
Yes. During the summer season, many itineraries combine Astore with Skardu via Deosai National Park.

Is Astore suitable for families?
Yes. Many attractions are suitable for family travel and nature tourism.
