Where is Skardu?
Skardu is located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of northern Pakistan.
Situated at approximately 2,230 meters above sea level, the town lies at the confluence of the Indus and Shigar Rivers and serves as the main gateway to Baltistan and the central Karakoram.
Travelers can reach Skardu by:
Domestic Flight
A spectacular flight from Islamabad offering magnificent views of the Himalaya, Karakoram, and Nanga Parbat.
Scenic Road Journey
Via the Karakoram Highway and Jaglot–Skardu Road, providing dramatic mountain scenery throughout the journey.

The History of Baltistan
Baltistan has long served as an important crossroads linking South Asia, Central Asia, Tibet, and China.
Over the centuries, traders, explorers, pilgrims, and travelers crossed these mountains, leaving behind a rich legacy of cultural exchange.
The region's unique identity reflects influences from Tibet, Central Asia, and the broader Silk Road world.
Today, visitors can still experience this distinctive heritage through Baltistan's architecture, traditions, language, cuisine, and historic sites.

Top Attractions in Skardu & Baltistan
Shigar Fort
One of Pakistan's finest heritage properties.
Dating back approximately 400 years, Shigar Fort was once the residence of the Raja of Shigar and has been beautifully restored.
Highlights include:
• Traditional architecture
• Historic exhibitions
• Cultural heritage
• Beautiful gardens
• Mountain views
The fort offers a fascinating introduction to Baltistan's royal history.

Khaplu Palace
Often described as one of Pakistan's most beautiful heritage sites.
Located in the picturesque town of Khaplu, this magnificent palace reflects the region's unique architectural traditions and cultural heritage.
Visitors can enjoy:
• Historic architecture
• Museum collections
• Scenic surroundings
• Traditional Balti culture

Shangrila Resort
One of Skardu's most famous attractions.
Located beside Lower Kachura Lake, Shangrila offers:
• Beautiful lake views
• Mountain scenery
• Relaxing atmosphere
• Excellent photography opportunities
The iconic airplane restaurant has become a symbol of tourism in Skardu.

Upper Kachura Lake
A pristine alpine lake surrounded by mountains and orchards.
The lake is renowned for:
• Crystal-clear water
• Boating opportunities
• Scenic walks
• Exceptional photography
It remains one of Baltistan's most picturesque locations.

Satpara Lake
Situated just outside Skardu, Satpara Lake is an important freshwater reservoir and one of the region's most scenic attractions.
Visitors can enjoy:
• Spectacular views
• Boating
• Photography
• Mountain landscapes

Deosai National Park
Known as the "Land of Giants," Deosai is one of the highest plateaus in the world.
During summer, the park transforms into a vast landscape of wildflowers, grasslands, streams, and snow-capped mountains.
Highlights include:
• Sheosar Lake
• Wildlife viewing
• Himalayan brown bears
• Alpine scenery
• Endless photographic opportunities
Deosai is widely considered one of Pakistan's most extraordinary natural attractions.

Sarfaranga Cold Desert
One of the world's highest cold deserts.
The golden sand dunes of Sarfaranga create a remarkable contrast against the surrounding mountains.
Popular activities include:
• Photography
• Jeep safaris
• Cultural excursions
• Scenic exploration

Manthoka Waterfall
Located in the Kharmang Valley, Manthoka Waterfall is among Baltistan's most beautiful natural attractions.
Its dramatic setting makes it a favorite destination for photographers and nature lovers.

K2 and the Karakoram
No discussion of Skardu is complete without mentioning K2.
Standing at 8,611 meters, K2 is the second-highest mountain in the world and one of the most legendary peaks in mountaineering history.
Skardu serves as the primary gateway for:
• K2 Base Camp Trek
• Gondogoro La Trek
• Broad Peak Expeditions
• Gasherbrum Expeditions
• Trango Towers
• Baltoro Glacier Treks
Even travelers who do not trek can appreciate the extraordinary mountain culture and heritage associated with these iconic peaks.
