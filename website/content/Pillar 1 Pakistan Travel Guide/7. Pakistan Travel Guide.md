Where is Pakistan?
Pakistan is located at the crossroads of South Asia, Central Asia, the Middle East, and China.
It shares borders with:
China 
India 
Afghanistan 
Iran 
The country's strategic location has shaped its history for thousands of years, making it a meeting point of civilizations, trade routes, religions, and cultures.

UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Pakistan
Pakistan is home to several remarkable UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
These include:
Lahore Fort & Shalimar Gardens
Masterpieces of Mughal architecture.
Taxila
Ancient center of learning and Gandhara civilization.
Takht-i-Bahi
Outstanding Buddhist monastery complex.
Mohenjo-daro
One of the world's oldest urban civilizations.
Makli Necropolis
One of the largest funerary sites in the world.
Rohtas Fort
A masterpiece of military architecture.
These sites offer fascinating insights into Pakistan's rich cultural history.

Major cultural regions include:
Punjab
Historic cities, agriculture, Sufi heritage, and Mughal architecture.
Sindh
Ancient civilizations, deserts, and Indus heritage.
Balochistan
Vast landscapes, tribal traditions, and coastal beauty.
Khyber Pakhtunkhwa
Mountain cultures, Gandhara heritage, and scenic valleys.
Gilgit-Baltistan
Silk Road history and spectacular mountain communities.
Kashmir
Traditional culture and alpine landscapes.
Each region offers distinct experiences and traditions.

The Silk Road in Pakistan
For centuries, Pakistan formed a vital section of the ancient Silk Road.
Historic routes passed through:
Taxila 
Gilgit 
Hunza 
Khunjerab Pass 
These routes connected South Asia with Central Asia and China, facilitating trade, cultural exchange, and the spread of ideas.
Today, travelers can still follow many of these historic pathways while enjoying some of the world's most dramatic mountain scenery.
