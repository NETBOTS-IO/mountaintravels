Expedition Overview
Passu Peak rises high above the famous village of Passu and the dramatic landscapes of Upper Hunza.
Overshadowed by the iconic Passu Cones, the mountain remains one of the most rewarding climbing objectives in the region.
Passu Peak
Offering challenging climbing, magnificent glacier scenery, and a genuine expedition atmosphere, Passu Peak attracts experienced mountaineers seeking a less-crowded Karakoram objective.
The mountain combines technical climbing with extraordinary views of the surrounding Karakoram ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Passu Peak crowded?
No. It remains a relatively little-climbed mountain compared to major expedition peaks.

Is Passu Peak technical?
Yes. Advanced mountaineering skills are required.

How long does the expedition take?
Typically 30–45 days.

What is the best climbing season?
June through August.

Can I combine this expedition with a Hunza Valley tour?
Yes. Many climbers spend additional time exploring Hunza and Upper Hunza before or after the expedition.
