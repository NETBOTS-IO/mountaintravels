Expedition Overview
Gasherbrum II is the 13th-highest mountain in the world and one of Pakistan's celebrated 8,000-meter peaks.
Located deep within the Karakoram near the Chinese border, Gasherbrum II rises majestically above the Baltoro Glacier and forms part of the famous Gasherbrum massif.
Known for its elegant shape and comparatively straightforward route, Gasherbrum II is frequently chosen by climbers seeking their first successful ascent of an 8,000-meter mountain.
Gasherbrum II
While often described as one of the more accessible 8,000ers, Gasherbrum II remains a serious high-altitude challenge requiring excellent fitness, mountaineering experience, and careful acclimatization.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Gasherbrum II suitable as a first 8,000-meter mountain?
Many climbers consider it one of the best introductory 8,000-meter peaks, though it remains a serious undertaking.

Is Gasherbrum II easier than K2?
Technically, yes. However, both mountains require extensive experience and preparation.

Can I combine Gasherbrum I and II?
Yes. Many climbers attempt both during the same expedition.

How long does the expedition take?
