Expedition Overview
Nanga Parbat is one of the world's fourteen 8,000-meter mountains and among the most famous peaks in mountaineering history.
Towering above the western Himalayas, Nanga Parbat rises dramatically from the valleys below and commands one of the most impressive mountain profiles on Earth.
Known globally as:
Nanga Parbat
the mountain has fascinated explorers, climbers, photographers, and adventurers for more than a century.
Its challenging terrain, tragic early history, and spectacular beauty earned it the famous nickname:
The Killer Mountain
Today, climbing Nanga Parbat remains one of mountaineering's greatest achievements.

Why Is Nanga Parbat Called the Killer Mountain?
Before its first ascent, numerous expeditions suffered tragedy on the mountain.
Its:
Technical terrain 
Avalanches 
Weather systems 
Isolation

Modern climbing techniques and improved logistics have greatly enhanced safety, but Nanga Parbat remains a serious expedition objective.

Why is it called the Killer Mountain?
The nickname comes from the mountain's difficult climbing history and early expedition tragedies.

Which route is most commonly climbed?
