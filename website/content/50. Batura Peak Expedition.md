Expedition Overview
Batura Peak is one of the highest mountains in Pakistan outside the famous 8,000-meter peaks.
Rising above the immense Batura Glacier in Upper Hunza, the mountain forms part of one of the most spectacular and remote regions of the Karakoram.
Batura Peak
Although less known than K2 or Nanga Parbat, Batura Peak is highly respected among experienced mountaineers and offers a true expedition experience in a remote wilderness setting.
For climbers seeking a serious high-altitude objective away from crowded routes, Batura Peak is one of Pakistan's finest challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Batura Peak crowded?
No. Batura Peak remains one of the less-frequented major mountains in Pakistan.

Is Batura Peak technical?
Yes. Climbers require advanced mountaineering skills and expedition experience.

How long does the expedition take?
Typically 35–50 days.

What is the best climbing season?
June through August.

Can this expedition be combined with Hunza Valley travel?
Yes. Many climbers combine the expedition with cultural touring in Hunza and Upper Hunza.
