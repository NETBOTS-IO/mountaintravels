Trek Overview
The Patundas Trek is one of the most scenic trekking routes in the Hunza region of northern Pakistan.
Located in Upper Hunza near the ancient Silk Road corridor, the trek leads through dramatic mountain landscapes, traditional Wakhi settlements, alpine pastures, and high-altitude viewpoints overlooking some of the Karakoram's most spectacular glaciers.
The highlight of the journey is the magnificent Patundas Plateau, a stunning alpine meadow situated above:
Batura Glacier 
Passu Glacier 
The panoramic views from Patundas are among the finest in Pakistan.

Trek Highlights
Patundas Plateau
The Crown Jewel of the Trek
Patundas is a vast alpine plateau offering incredible views of glaciers, mountains, and valleys.
The peaceful environment and dramatic scenery make it one of Pakistan's most rewarding trekking destinations.
Visitors enjoy:
Wide-open mountain vistas 
Wildflower meadows 
Glacier panoramas 
Outstanding photography 

Batura Glacier
One of the largest glaciers outside the polar regions.
The glacier stretches for more than 50 kilometers and dominates the landscape below Patundas.
The views from above are truly spectacular.

Passu Glacier
A magnificent glacier descending from the high peaks surrounding Upper Hunza.
Its icy formations provide dramatic contrasts against the green valleys and alpine meadows.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is the Patundas Trek difficult?
It is considered a moderate trek suitable for reasonably fit travelers.

Do I need previous trekking experience?
Previous hiking experience is beneficial but not essential.

What is Patundas famous for?
Patundas is famous for its spectacular views of the Batura and Passu glaciers and its stunning alpine plateau.

Can I combine it with a Hunza tour?
Yes. Most travelers visit Patundas as part of a broader Hunza Valley itinerary.

When is the best time to trek?
June through September generally offers the best conditions.
