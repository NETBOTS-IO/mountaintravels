Trek Overview
The Snow Lake Trek is one of the most remarkable trekking expeditions on Earth.
Deep within the remote Karakoram Mountains of northern Pakistan lies an immense glacial basin known as Snow Lake, a breathtaking high-altitude wilderness surrounded by towering peaks, glaciers, and some of the most isolated mountain landscapes in Asia.
The route follows the famous Biafo Glacier before reaching the magnificent Snow Lake basin and, in some itineraries, continuing across the Hispar Glacier to the Hunza region.
For experienced trekkers seeking solitude, exploration, and true expedition travel, Snow Lake offers an experience unlike any other.

What is Snow Lake?
Snow Lake is not actually a lake.
It is a vast glacial basin located at the junction of:
Biafo Glacier 
Hispar Glacier 
Together they form one of the largest glacial systems outside the polar regions.
The immense snow-covered basin stretches across the heart of the Karakoram and creates one of the most spectacular high-mountain landscapes in the world.
Early explorers considered Snow Lake one of the greatest geographical wonders of Asia.

Trek Highlights
Biafo Glacier
One of the longest glaciers outside the polar regions.
The glacier forms the gateway to Snow Lake and offers days of spectacular trekking through a pristine mountain environment.

Snow Lake Basin
The Heart of the Expedition
The immense snow-covered basin is one of the most awe-inspiring sights in the Karakoram.
Visitors encounter:
✓ Endless snowfields
✓ Giant glaciers
✓ Towering peaks
✓ Complete wilderness
✓ Extraordinary silence
Many trekkers consider this one of the greatest mountain landscapes on Earth.

Hispar Pass (Extended Route)
Some expeditions continue beyond Snow Lake and cross the famous Hispar Pass.
This extension links Baltistan with the Hunza region and creates one of the most epic glacier traverses in the world.

Hunza Valley Extension
Extended expeditions may conclude in the spectacular Hunza Valley, combining wilderness adventure with cultural exploration.

Historical Significance
The Snow Lake region played an important role in the exploration history of the Karakoram.
British explorers, survey teams, and mountaineers regarded the area as one of the most challenging and fascinating regions of High Asia.
Even today, the trek retains the spirit of early exploration.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Snow Lake harder than K2 Base Camp?
The challenge is different. Snow Lake involves longer glacier travel and greater remoteness, while K2 Base Camp generally involves more altitude exposure and larger expedition logistics.

Do I need previous trekking experience?
Yes. This trek is recommended for experienced trekkers.

Is Snow Lake crowded?
No. One of its greatest attractions is its isolation and solitude.

What is the best time to trek?
June through August generally provides the best conditions.

Can Snow Lake be combined with Hunza?
Yes. Extended expeditions can traverse the Hispar Glacier and finish in Hunza.
