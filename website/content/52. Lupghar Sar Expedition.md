Expedition Overview
Lupghar Sar is one of the most prominent mountains in the western Karakoram and a highly rewarding objective for experienced climbers seeking a remote expedition experience.
Situated in the mountains of Upper Hunza near the historic Silk Road corridor, the peak offers magnificent scenery, challenging climbing, and an atmosphere of genuine exploration.
Lupghar Sar
Far from crowded climbing destinations, Lupghar Sar provides mountaineers with the opportunity to experience the wild and lesser-visited side of the Karakoram.

Why Is Lupghar Sar Special?
Lupghar Sar lies close to one of Asia's most historic travel corridors.
For centuries, traders, explorers, and travelers moved through nearby routes connecting:
South Asia 
Central Asia 
Western China 
Today the region remains one of the least explored mountaineering destinations in Pakistan.
For climbers seeking solitude and adventure, Lupghar Sar offers a truly authentic expedition environment.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Lupghar Sar frequently climbed?
No. It remains one of the less-visited high peaks of the Karakoram.

Is the expedition technical?
Yes. Climbers require solid mountaineering experience and high-altitude capability.

How long does the expedition take?
Typically 28–40 days.

What is the best climbing season?
June through August.

Can the expedition be combined with tourism in Hunza?
Absolutely. Many climbers combine the expedition with cultural and sightseeing experiences in Hunza and Upper Hunza.
