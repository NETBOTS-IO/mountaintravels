Trek Overview
The Nanga Parbat Base Camp Trek is one of Pakistan's most rewarding mountain adventures.
This spectacular journey leads trekkers through the famous Fairy Meadows region to the base of:
Nanga Parbat
Standing at 8,126 meters, Nanga Parbat is the ninth-highest mountain on Earth and one of the most dramatic peaks in the Himalayas.
Known historically as the "Killer Mountain" because of the challenges faced by early mountaineering expeditions, Nanga Parbat today attracts trekkers, photographers, nature lovers, and adventurers from around the world.
The trek offers extraordinary mountain scenery without the extended commitment required for major Karakoram expeditions.

About Nanga Parbat
The Killer Mountain
Nanga Parbat has long held a legendary place in mountaineering history.
Key facts:
Height: 8,126 meters 
World's ninth-highest mountain 
Western anchor of the Himalayas 
One of the world's great mountaineering challenges 
The mountain's immense Rupal Face is among the highest mountain walls on Earth.

Trek Route Overview
Islamabad → Chilas → Raikot Bridge → Fairy Meadows → Beyal Camp → Nanga Parbat Base Camp → Return
