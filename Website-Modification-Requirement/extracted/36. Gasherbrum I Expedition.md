Expedition Overview
Gasherbrum I is one of the world's fourteen 8,000-meter mountains and among the most elegant peaks in the Karakoram.
Known historically as:
Gasherbrum I
the mountain rises to 8,080 meters and dominates the upper Baltoro region with its striking pyramid-shaped summit.
Although overshadowed in public awareness by K2, experienced mountaineers regard Gasherbrum I as one of the finest high-altitude climbing objectives in the world.
The expedition combines a spectacular approach through the Baltoro Glacier with a technically challenging ascent of one of the Karakoram's great giants.

What Does "Gasherbrum" Mean?
The name is often interpreted as deriving from local Balti words referring to a beautiful mountain.
Gasherbrum I certainly lives up to that reputation, standing among the most visually impressive peaks in High Asia.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is Gasherbrum I harder than Broad Peak?
Many climbers consider Gasherbrum I slightly more technical, though both are serious 8,000-meter expeditions.

Why is it called Hidden Peak?
The name was used by early explorers because the mountain was difficult to see from many viewpoints in the Karakoram.

Can Gasherbrum I and II be climbed together?
Yes. Many climbers attempt both peaks during a single expedition.

How long does the expedition take?
Typically 40–55 days.

What is the best climbing period?
July generally offers the strongest summit opportunities.
