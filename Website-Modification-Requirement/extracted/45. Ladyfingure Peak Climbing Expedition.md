Expedition Overview
Few mountains in Pakistan are as instantly recognizable as Ladyfinger Peak.
Rising dramatically above Karimabad and Hunza Valley, its slender rock spire forms one of the most distinctive mountain silhouettes in Asia.
Ladyfinger Peak
Part of the Ultar Massif, Ladyfinger Peak has fascinated climbers, photographers, and travelers for decades.
The mountain's unique shape, technical routes, and commanding position above Hunza make it one of Pakistan's most sought-after alpine climbing objectives.

Is Ladyfinger Peak technical?

Can beginners climb Ladyfinger Peak?
No. Prior mountaineering and climbing experience is strongly recommended.

How long does the expedition take?
Generally 15–25 days.

What is the best climbing season?
June through September.

Can this expedition be combined with a Hunza tour?
Absolutely. Many climbers combine their expedition with cultural and sightseeing experiences in Hunza Valley.
