The Eighth Wonder of the World
Stretching through some of the most spectacular mountain landscapes on Earth, the Karakoram Highway is one of the world's greatest road journeys.
Often called the Eighth Wonder of the World, this remarkable highway connects Pakistan and China through the heart of the Karakoram Mountains, following routes that have been used by traders, pilgrims, explorers, and travelers for centuries.
The modern highway largely follows the path of the ancient Silk Road, passing through dramatic valleys, historic settlements, towering peaks, glaciers, rivers, and some of the most breathtaking scenery anywhere on the planet.
For many travelers, a journey along the Karakoram Highway is not simply a road trip—it is an unforgettable expedition through history, culture, and nature.

What is the Karakoram Highway?
The Karakoram Highway (KKH) is an international highway linking Pakistan and China through the Khunjerab Pass.
It traverses:
Punjab 
Khyber Pakhtunkhwa 
Gilgit-Baltistan 
Xinjiang (China) 
The highway follows the Indus River for much of its route before entering the spectacular valleys of Gilgit, Nagar, Hunza, and Upper Hunza.
The route culminates at the Pakistan-China border at Khunjerab Pass, one of the highest paved international border crossings in the world.

Construction of the Karakoram Highway
The highway is one of the most ambitious engineering projects ever undertaken.
Construction began in the 1960s and required decades of work under extremely challenging conditions.
Engineers and workers faced:
Extreme altitude 
Harsh weather 
Landslides 
Glaciers 
Difficult terrain 
The completed highway stands as a symbol of cooperation, determination, and engineering excellence.
