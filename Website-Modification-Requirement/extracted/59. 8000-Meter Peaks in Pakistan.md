8000-Meter Peaks in Pakistan
Pakistan's Five Giants of the Karakoram & Himalaya
A Complete Guide to the Highest Mountains in Pakistan
Pakistan is one of the world's greatest mountaineering destinations and is home to five of the fourteen mountains on Earth that rise above 8,000 meters.
These legendary peaks attract elite climbers, mountaineers, researchers, photographers, and adventure travelers from around the world.
From the mighty K2 to the imposing slopes of Nanga Parbat, Pakistan's 8,000-meter mountains represent some of the most prestigious and challenging objectives in global mountaineering.

Pakistan and the World's 8000-Meter Mountains
There are only fourteen mountains on Earth that exceed 8,000 meters in elevation.
Five of those mountains are located in Pakistan.
This means Pakistan contains more than one-third of the world's 8,000-meter peaks, making it one of the most important mountaineering countries on the planet.

About K2
K2 is the second-highest mountain in the world and one of the most famous climbing objectives ever attempted.
Often referred to as "The Savage Mountain," K2 is renowned for its steep terrain, extreme weather, and demanding climbing conditions.
Many mountaineers regard K2 as the ultimate mountaineering challenge.

About Nanga Parbat
Known as the "Killer Mountain," Nanga Parbat dominates the western Himalaya and is one of the most visually impressive mountains in the world.
Its enormous south face, known as the Rupal Face, is among the highest mountain faces on Earth.

Why Nanga Parbat Is Famous
